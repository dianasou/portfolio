Greener Life

A sustainability app

Overview:

Greener Life is an app designed for iOS and Android to help people live an environmentally friendly lifestyle. It is for people with knowledge about sustainability at all levels.

Purpose:

The purpose of designing this app was to provide a platform with resources for people to make lifestyle changes towards a sustainable or greener life. This design was part of my CareerFoundry course.

Objective:

Problem -  The problem I aimed to solve was bridging the gap between the lack of resources and consistent motivation for users that are looking to live a greener life. It can be hard for users to entirely switch up how they are living at once.

Solution - I wanted to design a goal tracking app that is informative, motivating, and user-friendly. This app would guide them towards actions at different levels of difficulty that they can take at their own pace while reaching custom goals. Some key features in the app are allowing users to easily view customizable recommendations, a way to keep track of goals, and implementing a reward system with badges and progress updates to keep users motivated.

Role & Duration:

1 month

  • UI Designer
  • UX Designer

Tools:

  • Sketch
  • InVision

Process:

I compared the pros and cons of similar apps to find out what worked for users, and how other designers have approached creating a habit tracking app.


A user flow was created to map out how users could accomplish tasks.



Low-fidelity wireframes were sketched for iOS and Android. I used the iOS Human Interface Guidelines and Material Guidelines to inform me about design standards between device types to create a more consistent and user-friendly app.


I used Sketch to create mid-fidelity wireframes, which helped me determine the visual inconsistencies between my iOS and Android platforms.


I created a guide on the different mobile gestures for Greener Life. It showed me improvements I could make on the layout of certain screens and sizing of UI elements. I added additional screens I discovered were missing from the user flow.


Finally, I designed high-fidelity wireframes and created mockup prototypes in InVision for users to test. The most important feedback I looked for were ones that could improve the user’s experience with navigating the app and completing a task. Feedback about visual elements was also valued, and if some were conflicting I chose the majority opinion to decide whether to make changes or not.
